About the White House

The White House is the official residence and workplace of the President of the United States, located at 1600 Pennsylvania Avenue NW in Washington, D.C.

History

Construction began in 1792, with President John Adams moving in on November 1, 1800.

It survived the war of 1812.

Presidents Theodore Roosevelt and Harry Truman changed it a lot

Today, it stands as a symbol of where the president lives (we know his address)

Architecture

James Hoban designed it in the neoclassical style.

It has two gardens, the Rose Garden and Jacqueline Kennedy Garden.

It is bigger than my house 55000 square feet.

The East Room and Blue Room.

Quick Facts

Statistics

Contains 132 rooms, 35 bathrooms, and 6 levels.

Features 412 doors, 147 windows, and 28 fireplaces.

Employs over 90 full-time staff and numerous part-time workers.

Maintained by the National Park Service.
